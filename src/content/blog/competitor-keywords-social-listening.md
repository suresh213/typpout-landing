The absolute easiest customer to close is a prospect who is already paying for a solution in your category but is actively frustrated with it.

They have already gone through the education phase. They understand why they need the technology. They have allocated budget. The only thing they are missing is a better alternative.

In the past, catching these disgruntled buyers was a matter of luck. In 2026, you can automate this process using **social listening**. By monitoring specific competitor keywords and phrases across LinkedIn, X, and Instagram, you can identify buyers looking to switch and swoop in with a contextual pitch within minutes.

Here are the 15 competitor keywords and phrases every B2B SaaS GTM team should monitor.

---

## Niche 1: The "Alternative Seekers" (High Purchase Intent)

These keywords indicate a prospect is in active evaluation mode and wants direct recommendations from their network.

1. **"[Competitor] alternative"**
   * *Example*: *"Looking for a solid Apollo alternative that doesn't charge per seat. What is everyone using?"*
2. **"Switching from [Competitor]"**
   * *Example*: *"Currently switching from ZoomInfo. Need something with better direct dials in Europe."*
3. **"Anyone using [Competitor]?"**
   * *Example*: *"Anyone using Expandi? How is their API deliverability lately?"*
4. **"Better than [Competitor]"**
   * *Example*: *"Need a sales engagement tool that is better than Outreach for multi-channel tasks."*

---

## Niche 2: The "Frustration Signals" (Competitor Dissatisfaction)

These triggers catch buyers experiencing technical glitches, poor support, or billing issues with their current provider.

5. **"[Competitor] down" or "[Competitor] outage"**
   * *Example*: *"Is Lusha down for everyone else today or just me? GTM is at a complete standstill."*
6. **"[Competitor] support"**
   * *Example*: *"Been waiting 3 days for [Competitor] support to resolve an API issue. Awful."*
7. **"Frustrated with [Competitor]"**
   * *Example*: *"Genuinely frustrated with [Competitor's] UI updates. Simple tasks now take 5 clicks."*
8. **"[Competitor] pricing" or "[Competitor] contract"**
   * *Example*: *"Just got our renewal quote for [Competitor] and the price increased by 40%. Ridiculous."*

---

## Niche 3: The "Feature Gaps" (Specific Product Limitations)

These keywords catch buyers who want specific functionalities that their legacy tool does not provide.

9. **"[Competitor] doesn't"**
   * *Example*: *"Clay is amazing but [Competitor] doesn't do real-time social listening."*
10. **"Wish [Competitor] had"**
    * *Example*: *"Wish HubSpot had a native AI reply agent that booked calendar meetings autonomously."*
11. **"How to sync [Competitor]"**
    * *Example*: *"How to sync Apollo leads directly to Slack without using Zapier? It keeps breaking."*
12. **"[Competitor] limits"**
    * *Example*: *"Hitting the connection request limits on Expandi again. Need a safer workaround."*

---

## Niche 4: The "Buying Committee Inquiries" (Peer Validation)

These phrases catch decision-makers seeking social proof from their peers before committing to a contract renewal.

13. **"[Competitor] vs [Competitor]"**
    * *Example*: *"Evaluating Instantly vs Lemlist for our outbound email campaigns. Any strong opinions?"*
14. **"[Competitor] reviews"**
    * *Example*: *"Any honest reviews on Trigify's social intent triggers? Is the data clean?"*
15. **"Recommend tool for [Use Case]"**
    * *Example*: *"Can anyone recommend a tool for sourcing B2B leads from LinkedIn posts?"*
