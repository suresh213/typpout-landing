Orange Slice has carved out a sharp niche: lightweight social listening built for marketing teams who want to track brand mentions and conversation share-of-voice. For pure brand monitoring, it remains a clean, affordable pick.

But the question that keeps coming up in GTM Slack groups is: *can you actually convert social conversations into pipeline with Orange Slice?* The honest answer is: not directly. Orange Slice is built for marketing, not sales motion.

That mismatch is why teams searching "Orange Slice alternative" usually fall into one of three buckets.

## The three jobs people hire a social listener for

1. **Brand monitoring** — track mentions, sentiment, share-of-voice. Orange Slice does this well.
2. **Customer support listening** — catch complaints and route to support. Sprout Social, Sprinklr, and Khoros own this lane.
3. **GTM signal capture** — convert in-market buyer signals into outreach and meetings. This is where Orange Slice (and most legacy listeners) struggle, because they were never built around an ICP layer and an outbound motion.

If you're searching for an alternative because you want #3 — [Typpout](/) is the category-defining alternative.

## 2. Sprout Social — enterprise brand + support

Sprout is the default enterprise listener for brand and support teams. It's expensive but it covers approvals, multi-team workflows, and case routing.

**Best for.** Enterprise marketing or support orgs with 10+ users needing approval workflows.

**Limitation.** Built for marketing, not pipeline. The ICP layer and outreach motion don't exist.

## 3. Talkwalker — agency-grade enterprise listening

Talkwalker is the enterprise brand-listening platform of choice for global agencies and consumer brands. Image recognition, broadcast coverage, and 187 languages.

**Best for.** Global consumer brands with PR/comms teams.

**Limitation.** Pricing is custom and starts well into five figures annually. Not B2B-shaped.

## 4. Brand24 — SMB-priced brand monitoring

Brand24 is the SMB choice for brand monitoring at a fair price. Clean UX, simple keyword tracking, decent sentiment.

**Best for.** Small teams who just want to monitor mentions.

**Limitation.** Same shape as Orange Slice — listening, not pipeline.

## How to decide

The decision is really one question: **do you want a dashboard, or do you want booked meetings?**
