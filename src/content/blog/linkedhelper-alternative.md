LinkedHelper is one of the oldest LinkedIn automation tools on the market. It automates connection requests, messages, profile visits, endorsements, and group interactions. For teams that want to scale their LinkedIn activity without manual effort, it provides basic automation at a low price.

But in 2026, blind LinkedIn automation is increasingly counterproductive. LinkedIn's detection systems have improved significantly, and users have become adept at identifying automated outreach. Mass connection requests and generic messages damage your brand more than they help.

## Why teams move away from LinkedHelper

1. **Account safety risks.** LinkedIn is actively cracking down on automation tools. LinkedHelper runs as a browser extension that mimics human actions, which puts accounts at risk of restrictions.
2. **No targeting intelligence.** LinkedHelper automates actions but does not help you identify which people to target. You are automating volume, not precision.
3. **Generic outreach.** Automated connection requests and messages lack the context needed to start meaningful conversations.
