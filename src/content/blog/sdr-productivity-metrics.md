Most SDR teams measure the wrong things. Activity metrics, emails sent, calls made, connections requested, track effort, not effectiveness. A rep who sends 200 emails and books zero meetings had a busy day but not a productive one.

In 2026, with AI handling more of the volume work, the metrics that matter have shifted from activity to outcomes.

## Metrics to stop measuring

### Emails sent per day
This metric incentivizes volume over quality. A rep who sends 50 highly targeted messages to intent-matched buyers will outperform a rep who sends 300 template emails to a cold list.

### Calls made per day
With answer rates below 5%, tracking calls made is measuring effort, not effectiveness. The metric rewards dialing, not connecting.

### LinkedIn connections requested per week
Volume connection requests without context produce low acceptance rates and risk account restrictions.

## Metrics to start measuring

### Signals acted on per week
How many buying intent signals did the SDR (or AI system) identify and engage with? This measures the quality of the sourcing operation.

### Reply rate by signal type
Which categories of buying signals produce the highest reply rates? This data helps optimize the signal library and targeting.

### Cost per qualified meeting
Total SDR cost (salary + tools + overhead) divided by qualified meetings booked. This is the single most important efficiency metric.

### Time from signal to first touch
How quickly does the team engage after a buying signal appears? Speed matters enormously. [Typpout's](/) data shows reply rates drop from 24% to 9% within the first hour.

### Meeting show rate
Not all booked meetings happen. Tracking show rate distinguishes between meetings that are genuinely qualified and those that were booked but never attended.

### Pipeline value per SDR
Revenue in the pipeline attributable to each SDR (or AI system). This connects activity to business outcomes.

## How AI changes SDR metrics

When AI handles signal monitoring, initial outreach, and reply management, the human SDR's role shifts to complex qualification and relationship building. The metrics should shift accordingly:

* **AI metrics**: Signal volume, match rate, response time, reply rate, meetings booked
* **Human SDR metrics**: Meeting quality score, deal progression rate, pipeline value, close rate
