LinkedIn's Social Selling Index (SSI) is a score from 0 to 100 that measures how effectively you are using LinkedIn for social selling. It is based on four components, each scored up to 25 points:

1. **Establishing your professional brand** (0-25): How complete and compelling is your profile? Do you publish content? Are you building a recognizable personal brand?
2. **Finding the right people** (0-25): Are you connecting with relevant decision-makers? Are you using search and Sales Navigator effectively?
3. **Engaging with insights** (0-25): Are you sharing relevant content? Are you participating in conversations? Are you engaging with your network's posts?
4. **Building relationships** (0-25): Are you connecting with and strengthening relationships with key decision-makers?

## Why SSI matters

LinkedIn's data shows that sellers with SSI scores above 70 generate 45% more opportunities than those below 70. While correlation does not equal causation, the underlying behaviors that drive SSI, building a strong profile, connecting with the right people, and engaging thoughtfully, are all genuinely productive sales activities.

## How to improve each component

### Professional brand (establish)
* Complete every section of your LinkedIn profile with buyer-focused language.
* Add a professional headshot and a banner image that communicates what you help buyers achieve.
* Publish original content at least once per week.
* Get recommendations from customers and colleagues.

### Finding the right people
* Use LinkedIn search and Sales Navigator to identify decision-makers in your target accounts.
* Connect with people who match your ICP, not just anyone who will accept.
* Focus on quality connections over volume.

### Engaging with insights
* Comment thoughtfully on posts from your target buyers.
* Share industry news and add your own perspective.
* Respond to buying intent signals when you see them.

### Building relationships
* Maintain ongoing conversations with connections, not just one-time outreach.
* Engage with the same people repeatedly over time to build familiarity.
* Move conversations from public comments to DMs to meetings.
