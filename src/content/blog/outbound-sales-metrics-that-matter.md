In the era of traditional outbound sales, tracking performance was simple. You monitored the activity logs of your human Sales Development Representatives (SDRs):
* How many emails did they send today?
* How many cold calls did they dial?
* What was the open rate of the campaign?

In 2026, as B2B organizations transition to autonomous **AI SDRs** and signal-based prospecting, these legacy metrics become completely obsolete. 

An AI SDR can send 10,000 emails in a second, making "emails sent" a meaningless vanity metric. Similarly, open rates have become highly unreliable due to automated email security screening and security filters.

To build a high-performance sales machine, you must update your GTM dashboard to track unit economics, intent-to-meeting conversion, and pipeline velocity.

Here are the modern sales metrics that actually matter.

---

## 1. Signal-to-Outreach Ratio (Sourcing Precision)

This metric evaluates the quality and relevance of your lead sourcing triggers. It measures what percentage of the signals detected by your social listener are actually high-quality opportunities worth writing to.

$$\text{Signal-to-Outreach Ratio} = \left( \frac{\text{Signals Approved for Outreach}}{\text{Total Signals Detected}} \right) \times 100$$

* **Why it matters**: If your listener flags 1,000 posts but your team only approves 50 for outreach, your keyword filters are too broad, resulting in wasted research time. You want a high precision ratio (>70%), indicating that your automated sourcing is highly aligned with your Ideal Customer Profile.

---

## 2. Conversation-to-Meeting Booked Rate (Reply Efficiency)

Once a prospect replies to your initial warm message, this metric tracks how effectively your AI Reply Agent qualifies their interest and converts them into a scheduled calendar demo.

$$\text{Conversation-to-Meeting Rate} = \left( \frac{\text{Booked Meetings}}{\text{Total Warm Conversations Initiated}} \right) \times 100$$

* **Why it matters**: If you are generating dozens of replies but failing to book meetings, there is a bottleneck in your objection handling copy or your calendar booking flow. High-performing AI agents maintain a conversion rate of **15% to 25%** from warm conversation to booked demo.

---

## 3. Cost Per Booked Meeting (Unit Economics)

The ultimate metric for evaluating the efficiency of your sales channels. It measures the total financial cost incurred to secure a single qualified demonstration.

$$\text{Cost Per Booking} = \frac{\text{Total Software Subscription Cost + Lead Enrichment Fees}}{\text{Total Booked Meetings}}$$

* **Traditional Outbound**: Average cost per meeting ranges between **$350 and $600** (accounting for human salaries and expensive data stack tools).
* **AI SDR Outbound (Typpout)**: Average cost per meeting runs between **$15 and $40**, driven by low subscription costs and highly efficient signal-to-meeting conversion.

---

## 4. Lead-to-Opportunity Velocity (Speed-to-Lead impact)

This metric tracks the average number of hours it takes for an initial social intent signal to convert into a scheduled calendar opportunity.

* **Why it matters**: In modern B2B sales, speed is a competitive moat. When a prospect posts about a challenge, the vendor that responds and books a call within 10 minutes wins the deal 70% of the time. Tracking this velocity ensures your AI infrastructure is executing instantly.

---

## Setting Up Your Modern GTM Dashboard

To transition your sales operations team to this modern tracking model:

1. **Retire Vanity Metrics**: Stop reporting on *emails sent*, *profile visits*, or *open rates* to your board.
2. **Focus on Quality Indicators**: Build CRM reports in HubSpot or Salesforce that track **Signal Quality**, **Meeting Booking Efficiency**, and **Cost Per Lead (CPL)**.
3. **Optimize the Unit Economics**: Use the cost per booked meeting metric to allocate budget away from legacy contact databases and toward real-time social listening resources like [Typpout](/).

Stop measuring how hard your GTM tools are working. Start measuring how efficiently they are converting intent into closed-won revenue.
