Buffer has been a staple in the marketing stack for over a decade. It makes it easy to schedule posts across LinkedIn, Twitter (X), Facebook, and Instagram from a single dashboard. For content marketing teams looking to maintain a consistent publishing cadence, it is a simple and reliable tool.

But in 2026, B2B go-to-market (GTM) teams are facing a hard truth: **publishing content and hoping for inbound leads is no longer enough to hit pipeline targets**. 

If your main goal is to generate revenue, get meetings, and turn social media conversations into qualified pipeline, scheduling tools like Buffer are the wrong shape of software. You don't need a publishing schedule; you need an outbound sales motion.

This article compares publishing tools like Buffer with active social selling platforms to help you decide which tool fits your goals.

## The strategic shift: Inbound publishing vs. Outbound social selling

The difference between Buffer and a modern social sales platform comes down to inbound vs. outbound:

* **Buffer (Inbound Publishing)**: You write a post, schedule it, and publish it to your profile. You wait for prospects to see it, like it, and click a link to visit your website. It is a slow, marketing-driven build.
* **Social Selling (Outbound Execution)**: You set up listening criteria for your category. When a prospect posts about a problem you solve (e.g., *"anyone unhappy with Salesforce?"* or *"looking for a cold email tool"*), you reach out to them directly with a personalized message. It is proactive and sales-driven.
