LinkedIn Groups have fallen out of favor with many marketers because the platform deprioritized them in the algorithm. But for B2B social selling, groups remain a valuable source of concentrated buyer signals.

Groups bring together professionals who share interests in specific topics, industries, or tools. When someone posts a question in a relevant group, the intent signal is strong: they are actively seeking information or solutions.

## Finding high-value groups

Look for groups that match your buyer's interests:

* **Industry-specific groups**: Groups focused on your target industry (e.g., "SaaS Sales Leaders," "B2B Marketing Executives")
* **Tool-specific groups**: Groups centered around tools your buyers use or your competitors' products
* **Problem-specific groups**: Groups focused on challenges your product solves (e.g., "Cold Email Best Practices," "Sales Automation")

## Engagement strategy

### Do not pitch

The fastest way to get removed from a group is to post promotional content. Groups are communities, not advertising channels.

### Provide genuine value

Answer questions with specific, helpful information. Share frameworks, data, and experiences that help group members solve their problems. Build reputation as a knowledgeable contributor.

### Monitor for intent signals

Watch for posts that indicate buying intent: tool evaluation questions, frustration with current solutions, requests for recommendations. These are the signals that social selling is built on.

### Move conversations to DMs

When someone asks a question that relates to your product's value proposition, respond publicly with a helpful answer, then follow up via DM to continue the conversation in private.
