When B2B sales teams think about social selling, they think about LinkedIn first, X second, and rarely consider Instagram. This is a mistake that leaves a significant channel untapped.

Instagram has evolved far beyond its B2C origins. In 2026, B2B executives, founders, and decision-makers are active on Instagram for professional content. The platform's emphasis on visual storytelling, stories, and DMs creates engagement opportunities that are different from, and often complement, what LinkedIn and X provide.

## How B2B professionals use Instagram

### Founder and executive personal brands
Many B2B founders and executives maintain Instagram accounts where they share behind-the-scenes content, business insights, and industry perspectives. These accounts attract professional audiences who engage with business content.

### Company culture and employer branding
B2B companies use Instagram to showcase their culture, team, and values. This content attracts potential customers who discover the company through culture-focused posts.

### Product discovery through visual content
Software demos, workflow videos, and feature highlights perform well on Instagram. Buyers discover new tools through visual content in ways that text-based platforms do not enable.

### DM conversations
Instagram DMs are less formal and less saturated than LinkedIn DMs. B2B professionals who ignore LinkedIn DMs from strangers are sometimes more responsive to well-crafted Instagram messages.

## B2B buying signals on Instagram

* Comments on competitor product posts expressing interest or dissatisfaction
* Engagement with industry hashtags and business content
* Stories and posts about business challenges and tool evaluations
* DM conversations with brands and industry figures
