ChatGPT and similar general-purpose AI assistants have become part of many sales teams' workflows. They help with writing email drafts, researching companies, summarizing meeting notes, and brainstorming messaging angles. For these use cases, they are genuinely useful productivity tools.

But there is a gap between what ChatGPT can do for sales and what a dedicated social selling platform can do. Understanding this gap helps you allocate AI tools effectively.

## What ChatGPT does well for sales

### Email and message drafting
Give ChatGPT context about a prospect (their role, company, recent news) and it can generate solid first drafts of outreach messages. This saves 5-10 minutes per message compared to writing from scratch.

### Company research
ChatGPT can synthesize publicly available information about target companies: what they do, their market position, recent news, and potential pain points.

### Objection handling preparation
Provide common objections your team faces and ChatGPT can help develop response frameworks.

### Meeting preparation
Summarize a prospect's LinkedIn activity, recent company news, and competitive landscape before a discovery call.

## What ChatGPT cannot do

### Real-time social monitoring
ChatGPT does not monitor LinkedIn, X, or Instagram for buying intent signals. It responds to prompts, but it does not proactively listen for opportunities.

### Automated outreach execution
ChatGPT can draft messages, but it cannot send them, track responses, or follow up automatically.

### ICP matching
ChatGPT has no mechanism for evaluating whether a social signal comes from a buyer who matches your ideal customer profile.

### Conversation management
ChatGPT cannot autonomously manage ongoing sales conversations, qualify leads, or book meetings.

### Real-time intent detection
ChatGPT's training data has a cutoff. It cannot see what buyers are posting right now on social platforms.
